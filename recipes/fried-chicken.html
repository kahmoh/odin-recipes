Fried Chicken

This fried chicken batter yields the crispiest, spiciest, homemade fried chicken I have ever tasted! It is equally good served hot or cold and has been a picnic favorite in my family for years.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil for frying
  • 4 ¼ cups and 1 tablespoon and ⅞ teaspoon and ⅒ teaspoon all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1 ½ tablespoons garlic salt
  • 1 tablespoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon poultry seasoning
  • 1 ½ cups beer, or as needed
  • 2 egg yolks, beaten
  • 1 teaspoons salt
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 (3 pound) whole chicken, cut into pieces

Directions

    Step 1

  • Heat oil in a deep-fryer to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).

    • Step 2

  • Mix together 3 cups flour, garlic salt, 1 tablespoon pepper, paprika, and poultry seasoning in a medium bowl.

    • Step 3

  • Stir together 1 1/2 cups beer, remaining 1 1/3 cups flour, egg yolks, salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper in a separate bowl; thin mixture with more beer if batter is too thick.

    • Step 4

  • Moisten chicken pieces with a bit of water, then dip in the dry mixture. Shake off excess and dip in the wet mixture, then dip in the dry mixture once more./li>

    Step 5

  • Fry chicken pieces in hot oil until well-browned and internal temperature reaches 165 degrees F (74 degrees C), 15 to 18 minutes.

    • Step 6

  • Remove chicken pieces and drain on paper towels before serving.
